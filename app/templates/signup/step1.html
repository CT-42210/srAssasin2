Step 1: Game Rules

Important: Please read and acknowledge the game rules before proceeding.

Game Overview

Senior Assassin is a game of stealth, strategy, and survival. Each team is assigned another team as their target. The goal is to "eliminate" all members of your target team using water-based weapons. The last team standing wins.

Teams

  • Teams can consist of either one or two players.
  • All team members must be seniors at our high school.
  • Once teams are formed, they cannot be changed.
  • Each team must have a team name and photo for identification.

Game Rounds

  • The game is divided into multiple rounds.
  • At the beginning of each round, teams are assigned a new target.
  • Rounds have a specific start and end time, which will be announced.
  • If a team is eliminated but their team has at least one surviving player, they will be revived at the start of the next round.

Elimination Rules

  1. Permitted weapons: Water guns, water balloons, and other water-based tools.
  2. To eliminate a player: You must hit them with water and say "You've been eliminated" or similar phrase.
  3. Video proof: All eliminations must be recorded on video as proof.
  4. Safe zones:
    • School property during school hours
    • Places of employment during work hours
    • Inside vehicles
    • Bathrooms
    • Religious services
    • School-sponsored events
  5. Team elimination: A team is eliminated when all its members have been eliminated.

Safety Guidelines

  1. Never trespass on private property.
  2. Never engage in dangerous behavior (e.g., chasing in traffic, climbing buildings).
  3. Water weapons should not be visible in public places where they might cause alarm.
  4. No eliminations while driving or that would distract a driver.
  5. Be respectful of bystanders and public spaces.

Sportsmanship

  1. Be honest about eliminations.
  2. Accept eliminations gracefully.
  3. Vote fairly on kill confirmations.
  4. No harassment or intimidation of other players.
  5. No destruction of property or disruptive behavior.
